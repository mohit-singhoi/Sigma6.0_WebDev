Ozymandias

by percy Bysshe Shelley

I met a traveller from an antique land,
Who said-"Two vast and trunklesss legs of stone
stand in the desert . . . near them, on tyhe stand,
Half sunk a shattered visage lines, whose frown,
And wrinkled lip, and sneer of cold command,
Tell that its sculptor well those lifeless things,
The hand that mocked them, and the heart that fed;
And on the pedestal , these words appear:
My name is Ozymandis, king of kings;
Look on my Works , ye Mighty, and despair!
nothing besides remains. Round the decay
Of that colossal wreck, boundless and bare
The lone and level stands stretch far away "
